About This Site

Pulaski doesn't make many national headlines. It doesn't have a famous skyline or a major airport. What it has is something harder to manufacture — a real town with real people who have been building something here for generations.

I grew up in Pulaski. I rode my bike on Lake Street, was a bat boy at Calfee Park, and have watched this town change over decades. I left to serve in the Army and came back. That gives me a particular kind of investment in what happens here.

LinksPulaski is the hyperlocal companion to LinksNRV — zoomed all the way in on the town at the heart of the New River Valley. The businesses on Main Street. The decisions made at Planning Commission. The things worth celebrating and the things worth questioning.

Written by someone who lives here, serves here, and cares about what this town becomes.
